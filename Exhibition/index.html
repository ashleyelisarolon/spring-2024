Sound Scape

Curated by Ashley Elisa Rolon

The artworks "The Wilderness Downtown", "Just A Reflektor", and "The Johnny Cash Project" are innovative examples of interactive and participatory media in the realm
of music videos. They all push the boundaries of traditional music video experiences by incorporating user interactivity and personalization. Each project showcases a unique
approach to engaging the audience, whether it's through the integration of Google Maps and Street View, real-time visual effects using webcams and smartphones, or
collaborative creation of a music video frame. Thematically, they explore the intersection of technology, music, and individual experiences, offering viewers a chance to
actively participate in the storytelling process. These artworks stand as significant milestones in the evolution of interactive media within the music industry.


The Wilderness Downtown, 2010, Written and Directed by Chris Milk.
The interactive music video for Arcade Fire's track "We Used to Wait" integrates Google Maps and Street View to tailor the experience to the user's childhood address.



http://thewildernessdowntown.com/


Just A Reflektor, 2013, by Arcade Fire.
This interactive music video for Arcade Fire's song "Reflektor" allows users to interact with the visuals using their webcam or smartphone, creating dynamic effects and
altering the narrative in real-time. This project is no longer active.



https://experiments.withgoogle.com/just-a-reflektor


The Johnny Cash Project, 2010, is a collaboration between Aaron Koblin and Chris Milk.
A web-based painting tool was created for visitors to create and share their vision for a single music video frame. These portraits were strung together and played in sequence
over the song. Site visitors can pause the video at any point to see that frame come to life. This project is completed and no longer active.



https://www.radicalmedia.com/work/the-johnny-cash-project
